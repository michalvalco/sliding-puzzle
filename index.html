Everything you need to know

Game manual

The whole game in a minute — no reading required to start.

  1. 1

    Pick a mode & size

    Choose a Picture puzzle (rebuild a photo) or a Number puzzle, then a grid: 3×3 to ease in, up to 5×5 for a real challenge. Harder sizes unlock as you win.

  2. 2

    Slide the tiles

    Click a tile next to the empty space, or use the arrow keys, to slide it in. Rebuild the picture (or put 1–N in order). The board is always solvable — you can’t get stuck.

  3. 3

    Win, and keep going

    Finish to earn a victory card, unlock the next difficulty, and grow your stats. Come back for the Daily Challenge and keep your streak alive.

Handy to know

  • Stuck? Picture mode has a hint that shows the finished image.
  • Misclick? Undo up to five moves, or reset the board.
  • Make it yours. Add your own photos and crop them in-game.
  • Comfort first. Switch theme, split music/SFX volume, or turn on reduced motion in Settings.

Is it safe? Why does Windows warn me?

The build isn’t code-signed (certificates are pricey for a free indie game), so Windows SmartScreen shows a blue “unrecognized app” notice the first time you run it. That’s expected — click More info → Run anyway. There’s no installer and no account, and nothing is uploaded: your photos and progress stay on your computer.

  1. 1

    Download & unzip

    Grab the .zip above and extract it anywhere.

  2. 2

    Run the game

    Double-click SlidingPuzzle.exe. First time, Windows SmartScreen may ask — choose More info → Run anyway (it’s an unsigned indie build).

  3. 3

    Play

    That’s it — your progress saves next to the game. No installer, no internet needed.
